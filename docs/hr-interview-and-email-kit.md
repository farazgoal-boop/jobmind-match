## Junior / Fresh Developer Interview Answers

### 1. You do not have formal company experience. Why should we consider you?
Although I may not have long formal company experience, I have built 3 production applications that solve real-world problems. This means I understand how to take a project from idea to working product. I can contribute with practical development skills from day one and I am ready to learn quickly in a team environment.

### 2. How are you different from other junior developers?
Many junior developers have only tutorial or sample project experience. I have built complete applications focused on actual business use cases, including property management, business operations, and AI-powered job matching. That gives me stronger practical understanding of backend systems, workflows, and problem solving.

### 3. What have you learned by building projects yourself?
I learned how to break large problems into smaller modules, choose an appropriate tech stack, design APIs and databases, handle file and document workflows, and focus on usability rather than only code completion. I also learned the importance of testing each workflow step-by-step.

### 4. How do you learn new technologies?
I learn by understanding the fundamentals first, then applying them in small practical examples, and finally using them in real project features. This is how I improved in Flask, FastAPI, SQLAlchemy, and related tools.

### 5. What kind of team are you looking for?
I want to join a team where I can work on real products, learn from experienced developers, improve my engineering discipline, and contribute through consistent practical work.

### 6. Are you comfortable starting with a junior role?
Yes. I am fully comfortable starting with a junior role because my goal is to enter a strong working environment, contribute effectively, and grow through real product experience.

### 7. What motivates you as a developer?
I enjoy solving practical problems with software. It is motivating for me when an application becomes useful in real life, improves workflow, or saves time for users or businesses.
